Recirculating Water Bath │ Capstone Project

Background

Built for Dr. Sravya Tekumalla’s research lab, this custom water bath was designed to support high-precision thermal testing in biomedical applications involving advanced metals and additive manufacturing.

Objectives

  • Maintain temperatures from ambient to 60°C for up to 2 months
  • Hold 16–22 test tubes with custom stainless steel trays
  • Ensure ±1°C thermal stability
  • Prioritize user-friendliness, safety, and low energy consumption

Engineering Approach

  • Arduino-based PI control loop with a 1500W heater and high-temperature pump
  • CFD-optimized internal flow using Siemens NX for uniform temperature distribution
  • Modular aluminum frame with in-house fabricated stainless steel panels
  • Intuitive user interface with 3.5” display and four-button control
  • Custom sealing strategy using bonded seals, gaskets, and specialty fittings

Results & Takeaways

  • Achieved ±0.1°C steady-state accuracy
  • Delivered a fully functional, lab-ready system under $1,200
  • Selected as the top design out of 20+ teams in the 2025 Capstone Showcase

This project strengthened our capabilities in thermal system design, CFD modeling, embedded control, and hands-on fabrication—highlighting the value of user-centered thinking and creative problem-solving under constraints.

Heat Transfer Test Rig | Accent Refrigeration Systems Ltd.

Background

This project was completed in collaboration with Accent Refrigeration to support design optimization of their snow-melting coil systems used in ice arena snow pits. The team was tasked with developing a test rig capable of measuring the heat transfer performance of submerged copper coil heat exchangers under real-world conditions.

Objectives

  • Provide standardized, repeatable testing for multiple coil designs
  • Operate within 10–35°C heated fluid range and 0–ambient°C bath conditions
  • Measure flow, pressure, and temperature with ±0.5°C accuracy
  • Enable data-driven design improvements for future coil iterations

Engineering Approach

  • Designed a closed-loop fluid circuit with a 40 kW immersion heater and a 1 hp pump circulating glycol solution through nine submerged copper coils
  • Integrated sensors for temperature, pressure, flow, ambient air, and bath level
  • Included a bottom-mounted agitator to maintain uniform bath mixing
  • Delivered full documentation: P&ID, one-line electrical diagram, CAD layout, and detailed component sizing
  • Heat transfer modeled as a resistance network, with performance estimated using LMTD and Dittus–Boelter equations

Results & Takeaways

  • Achieved a feasible and costed system design with a total estimated budget of $18,439 CAD
  • Analytical plots confirmed that increased flow rates significantly improve heat transfer, while agitator effects were less pronounced
  • Delivered a comprehensive build package ready for prototype development and experimental validation

This project strengthened our understanding of system-level design, instrumentation, and technical documentation. It highlighted the value of working closely with industry clients and balancing theoretical design with real-world application.

CFD/FEA Bike Frame Optimization

Background

This project aimed to aerodynamically and structurally optimize the frame of an Apollo Volare performance road bike using advanced FEA and CFD techniques. Targeted at competitive cyclists, the design focused on reducing aerodynamic drag while ensuring structural reliability under realistic loading, including crosswind exposure.

Objectives

  • Reduce aerodynamic drag using CFD-guided geometry changes
  • Maintain structural integrity under rider weight via static FEA
  • Validate performance across crosswind angles (0°–20°)
  • Preserve OEM compatibility while enabling high-performance upgrades and manufacturability

Engineering Approach

  • Conducted baseline FEA and CFD using mesh convergence to assess stress concentrations under distributed loads
  • Performed steady and transient CFD simulations at 40 km/h with 4 wind angles using a Realizable k-ε turbulence model
  • Iteratively transitioned from circular to elliptical (4.5:1) and teardrop profiles to reduce flow separation and wake drag
  • Performed crosswind analysis at yaw angles of 10°, 15°, and 20°
  • Optimized mesh refinement around high-gradient regions to balance simulation fidelity and runtime

Results & Takeaways

  • Achieved a 31% reduction in drag coefficient (1.4 → 0.97) through aerodynamic re-shaping
  • Final design retained low frame stress levels well below yield, despite thinner cross-section
  • Crosswind testing showed up to 50% reduction in drag force compared to the baseline under 10–20° yaw angles

This project sharpened our ability to integrate FEA and CFD for real-world optimization. It reinforced the importance of turbulence modeling, mesh strategy, and geometry iteration in pushing performance without compromising structural integrity.

CO₂ Pipeline Cost & Exergy Analysis

Performed exergy analysis of compressors and pumps to quantify system efficiencies for CO₂ transport via pipeline, using supercritical fluid properties. (Mech450c)

Calculated pipeline capital and operating costs every 250 km, showing $43.22/tCO₂ total cost under 45Q tax credit scenarios, and compared to rail and shipping alternatives.

Optimized compressor design for minimal energy penalty, integrating high-pressure multi-stage configuration, delivering >80% capture-to-transport efficiency.

Synthesized techno-economic trade-offs, highlighting pipeline feasibility at scale while addressing regulatory and land-use barriers for CCS deployment.

Mold Design & Manufacturing

Designed a two-part HDPE mold for a bicycle pedal using Mastercam and Vericut to simulate CNC toolpaths, optimizing feed rates and stepovers for surface finish. (MECH 460)

Developed four NC programs, varying tool types (ball, end, torus mills) and parameters, then used Vericut to analyze gouges & cusps to select the optimal toolpath for precision and speed.

Machined molds on CNC mills using HDPE blocks, achieving complex curved pockets and fillets with minimal manual finishing, validated by Vericut analysis to eliminate collisions.

Final parts exhibited high dimensional accuracy and surface quality, demonstrating mastery of toolpath strategy and CNC process planning for advanced manufacturing labs.

Energy-Efficient Grocery Store HVAC

Optimized building envelope for a Sol Duc Village convenience store: R-30 walls, green roof, double pane windows, reducing heat transfer by 60% vs. baseline. (MECH 442)

Performed hand and eQUEST load calculations for heating/cooling, selecting AHU capacity, diffusers, and duct layouts to meet indoor comfort at 21°C/40% RH.

Compared optimized vs. unoptimized cases: envelope improved summer loads by 55%, infiltration improvements dominated winter savings.

Cost analysis and future work recommended integration with district geothermal heating and advanced control strategies for net-zero operations.

Hybrid Tilt-Rotor VTOL

Conceptual design for BC Wildfire Service: hybrid-electric tilt-wing VTOL with 1 MW turbogenerator + 912 kW turboshaft + Li-S batteries. (MECH 475)

Performed initial sizing: MTOM 2733 kg for 8 passengers. Stability analyses confirmed positive longitudinal and directional stability across mission phases.

Used XFLR5 and analytical methods for wing/tail airfoil selection (NACA 23018 for wing, 0015 for tail), achieving a compact 11 m span with high-lift configuration.

Emissions & cost estimates: development cost $800–900 M USD, per-unit $10–15 M. Powertrain offers smoke-intolerant flight modes and low-noise operations near communities.
