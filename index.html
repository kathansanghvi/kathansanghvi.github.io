Recirculating Water Bath (Capstone Project)

Background

Developed for UVic’s thermal engineering labs to support biomedical experiments requiring ±0.1°C precision, replacing costly commercial baths.

Objectives

Build a self-contained water bath with stainless steel tank, PI-controlled heating, and internal recirculation to maintain ±0.1°C accuracy from ambient to 95°C.

Design & Engineering Approach

CAD and CFD in Siemens NX refined inlet/outlet geometry for uniform flow. Custom Arduino-based PI control tuned to ±0.05°C. Stainless steel fabrication with TIG welding.

Challenges

Preventing thermal stratification, tuning PI controller to avoid wind-up, and ensuring weld integrity with effective insulation.

Results

Achieved ±0.05°C stability over 1 hour. Validated via IR scans and manual sampling. Awarded Marianne Black Award for Best Design 2025.

Heat Transfer Test Rig

Last week, my team and I presented our Design of Thermofluids project at UVic! We designed a test rig for Accent Refrigeration Systems Ltd to evaluate the heat transfer performance of submerged copper coil heat exchangers used for melting arena ice shavings.

Final Design

  • 1 HP circulation pump and 40 kW immersion heater
  • Heated propylene glycol loop connected to submerged copper coils (1" CTS Type K, OD 1.125", ID 0.995")
  • Fully instrumented for real-time monitoring: flowmeter, pressure transducers, and RTDs
  • Complete engineering documentation: P&ID, one-line electrical schematic, CAD drawings
  • Pump and agitator sized based on Reynolds number (Re=1400–2800) to ensure turbulent mixing
  • Environmental and structural guidelines for reproducible testing in lab conditions

Technical Details

Thermodynamics: LMTD method for coil heat transfer, validated within ±5% of theoretical values.

Fluid Mechanics: Pump head calculations (14.8 m), EPDM tubing selection per Darcy-Weisbach analysis.

Controls & Data Acquisition: DAQ-ready layout, voltage/current monitoring of heater, and agitator sequencing to prevent stratification.

Hybrid Tilt-Wing VTOL Aircraft

Background

Concept for BC Wildfire Service, enabling runway-independent transport of crews to remote wildfire zones.

Objectives

Design a 10-passenger hybrid-electric tilt-wing for VTOL and efficient cruise, balancing range, payload, and speed.

Design & Engineering Approach

AHP used to select tilt-wing. CAD in SolidWorks; aerodynamic sizing via XFLR5; hybrid propulsion via mass/balance ADT inputs.

Challenges

Transition stability between hover and cruise, weight distribution of batteries vs engine.

Results

Final design met operational requirements; complete design package delivered to stakeholders.

CFD/FEA Road Bike Frame

Background

Optimized a road bike frame for competitive cycling, targeting aerodynamic improvement without losing structural integrity.

Objectives

Reduce drag by 30% and ensure stresses under rider loading stay below yield limits.

Design & Engineering Approach

Siemens NX CFD with Realizable k-ε; FEA under static loads (seat, handlebar, pedal); iterative geometry refinement to elliptical/teardrop profiles.

Challenges

Balancing aerodynamic shapes with manufacturability and rider ergonomics.

Results

30% drag reduction confirmed; maximum stress < 25 MPa, ready for prototype fabrication.

Geothermal HVAC Modeling

Background

Modeled HVAC for a grocery site on geothermal district heating to reduce energy use and carbon footprint.

Objectives

Optimize duct layout, insulation, and equipment to achieve 20–30% EUI reduction over baseline.

Design & Engineering Approach

Used eQUEST and psychrometric analysis for load profiles; ASHRAE-compliant zonal diffusers; green roof integration for summer cooling.

Challenges

Coordinating complex duct routing within existing structural constraints and minimizing infiltration.

Results

Design met comfort and performance goals; validated for geothermal system integration.

CNC Mold Design

Background

CNC-machined HDPE mold for bicycle pedal in a manufacturing course, evaluating toolpath impacts on finish and time.

Objectives

Compare CAM strategies (scallop, raster, hybrid) to balance surface quality and cycle time.

Design & Engineering Approach

Mastercam toolpaths validated in Vericut; machined on Tormach mill; varied stepover, RPM, and tool sizes.

Challenges

Managing HDPE chip welding and tool wear in tight radii.

Results

Produced molds with distinct finish profiles; documented best practices for rapid mold production.

CO₂ Pipeline Analysis

Background

Techno-economic modeling for long-haul CO₂ transport from capture sites to sequestration, assessing costs and exergy losses.

Objectives

Determine optimal pipeline diameter, pressure, and booster spacing to minimize lifetime cost and energy loss.

Design & Engineering Approach

Excel & EES used for real-fluid properties; NPV/IRR over 30 years; 500 km scenario with 3 booster stations.

Challenges

Ensuring supercritical phase stability over elevation changes and cost assumptions under market variability.

Results

Proposed cost-effective design with >90% exergy retention and positive economic metrics for 30-year operation.
